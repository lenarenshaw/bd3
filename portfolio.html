USABILITY REDESIGN

Beautifying and simplifying Bagel Gourmet Cafe's Website


The Problem

Bagel Gourmet Cafe is a very popular estabilishment servicing the local East Providence community's bagel needs. It's family owned and operated, and has been in business forever. However, its website is currently cluttered, unintuitive, and looks like it hasn't been updated since the mid-nineties. It was in serious need of a full redesign.


The Research

I posted an original design of this project here, and got a lot of feedback about what users wanted. Originally, I had a home, contact, location, and photo pages, but I learned that these could all be combined into one to reduce number of clicks. I also learned that left justified text is nice and the font looks outdated. I used this information to improve my design.


The Solution

The redesign consisted of creating simple wireframes for the two key screens: the home and menu pages. I reformatted the website to be continuously scrolling, so the entire website loads as one full page and the navigation bar links scroll to different parts of the page.

The Homepage

I simplified the homepage by eliminating unnecessary clutter. I created a header with useful information, like address and telephone number. The navigation bar is now on the top and quite clear and easy to use, and the homepage contains a small introduciton to the cafe and an image.


The Menu

On the original website, the menu page was hard to navigate beacuse it was poorly formatted and extremely long. I fixed this here by trying to condense information without making it look cluttered, and keeping everything in two columns all the way down the page. That way, it wasn't as long and seemed more organized.


Conclusion and Feedback

I learned that the iterative design process is crucial to creating a good website, and getting constant feedback from others is essential throughout every step of the process. I also think that the reddit design critiques community is really great and helpful for assisting me in becoming better at user design!
